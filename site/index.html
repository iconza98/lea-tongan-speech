Why this matters

Every language deserves to be heard by the future.

Voice assistants, captions, and translation tools are trained on languages with millions of hours of recordings. Tongan has almost none. The result: the technology our children grow up with can't speak, read, or understand the language of their grandparents — and no company has an incentive to fix it. So we're doing it ourselves, in the open.

Under-served, not unimportant

Mainstream speech tech skips smaller languages. An open corpus is the only way Tongan gets a seat at the table.

Built by the diaspora

Recorded by fluent, native speakers across every island and community — so the dialects and diacritics stay true.

Open forever

Released under a permanent open licence so anyone — researchers, teachers, app makers — can build on it, with credit.

How it works

One community corpus. Three open tools.

Every consented recording joins a single open dataset. That corpus trains a family of freely-shared models — each one a tool your community can actually use.

Your voice
Open corpus
Three models

Text-to-speech

Read any Tongan text aloud in a natural voice — for learning apps, accessibility, and preservation.

Speech-to-text

Turn spoken Tongan into written Tongan — captions, transcripts, and search for a language that had none.

Tongan → English

Bridge the gap for a generation that understands more than it can read — translation grounded in real speech.

How to contribute

Three minutes. Three simple steps.

No account, no app to install. If you speak Tongan, you can help — right from your browser.

STEP 01

Give consent

Read the plain-language terms and confirm you're happy to share your recording under an open licence.

STEP 02

Read aloud

A Tongan phrase appears. Tap record, say it naturally, and listen back — as many phrases as you like.

STEP 03

Submit

Your clip is reviewed, then published to the open dataset — helping train tools for everyone.
